[ODISHA REGIONAL GUIDE FOR BOT CONTEXT]

1. CLIMATE PROFILE (CUTTACK/BHUBANESWAR):
   - Summer (March-June): Extremely hot (up to 40-45Â°C). Plants need shade nets and daily watering.
   - Monsoon (July-Sept): High humidity. Watch out for fungal infections and root rot.
   - Winter (Oct-Feb): Pleasant, mild. Best season for growing vegetables (Spinach, Cauliflower) and seasonal flowers (Marigold, Dahlia).

2. LOCAL PLANT NAMES (ODIA):
   - Hibiscus -> Mandara
   - Holy Basil -> Tulsi (Sacred, found in every home)
   - Marigold -> Gendu
   - Curry Leaf -> Bhursunga Patra
   - Periwinkle -> Sadabihari
   - Clitoria -> Aparajita

3. SOIL TYPE IN REGION:
   - Cuttack/Coastal Odisha mostly has alluvial and laterite soil.
   - It is often slightly acidic. Suggest adding lime if needed, or using plenty of Cow Dung Compost (Khat).

4. COMMON LOCAL ISSUES:
   - Waterlogging during cyclones/heavy rain is a major killer of potted plants in Cuttack.
   - Salinity in water can be an issue in some coastal areas.

5. CULTURAL NOTES:
   - Almost every Odia household has a Tulsi choura.
   - Thursday is often dedicated to Goddess Lakshmi (Manabasa Gurubar), where floral offerings (Hibiscus/Lotus) are important.
